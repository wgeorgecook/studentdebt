President Biden partially fulfilled his campaign promise to forgive at minimum $10,000 for each American with student debt by limiting who qualifies for forgiveness.

With the repayment deadline looming, President Biden announced student loan forgiveness for borrowers making up to $120k. This order forgives $10k for single people, and $20k for Pell Grant recipients. Loan balances remaining will start repayment in October 2023.

See full announcement details on the Federal Student Aid website.

The Department of Education announced on September 29th that about 800,000 borrows with FFEL loans are not eligible for forgiveness.

The student loan forgiveness application launched officially on October 17th.

A Trump-appointed federal judge in Texas halted the Biden administration's loan forgiveness program on November 10th, 2022. The plaintiff in the case had nearly $48,000 in PPP loans forgiven.

This supreme court case may hault blanket forgiveness, and payments are guaranteed to resume in October 2023.
